Mara followed the numbers into abandoned tunnels beneath the harbor. Machines slept there, ribbed with rust, dreaming of instructions. The coordinates pulsed faster, mapping her thoughts, predicting choices. She reached a door that remembered her name. It opened to a chamber spinning with stars, each a frozen moment. A voice spoke from everywhere, calm and immense. It said Earth was an experiment paused too long. Resume, it asked, or be archived? Outside, tides obeyed new math, and distant sirens sang to empty streets alone. Mara hesitated, feeling history lean closer, waiting for permission from one fragile human with shaking hands.
